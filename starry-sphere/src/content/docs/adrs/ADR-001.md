title: "ADR-001: Hybrid Architecture for GreenGrant"
description: "An architectural decision record describing the choice of a hybrid architecture for the GreenGrant system."
---

- Status: Active


## In the context of
- the GreenGrant nationwide chocolate inventory platform, part of the Zamunda Digital Renaissance (ZDR)
- the subsystem responsible for ingesting, normalizing, and processing chocolate manufacturers’ inventory data

---

## Facing the need for
- ingestion of inventory data from heterogeneous sources and protocols (FTP, email, HTTP APIs, future channels)
- handling multiple data formats and evolving schemas (CSV, XML, JSON)
- serving a growing number of manufacturers (currently ~50, with long-term growth expected)
- displaying the most up-to-date possible inventory information despite imperfect internet connectivity
- traceability of inventory ingestion, including access to current inventories and previous ingestion attempts
- high availability, as delayed or lost inventory data reduces the usefulness of the platform
- cost-efficient, well-documented operation aligned with government and climate-friendly constraints
- future extensibility toward purchase functionality without redesigning the ingestion core

---

## We decided for
- a Hybrid Architecture consisting of:
  - a modular monolith for core domain logic, persistence, and web presentation
  - an Event-Driven Architecture (EDA) for inventory ingestion and asynchronous processing

---

## Achieving
- message-queue-based buffering that ensures inventory data is safely stored from the moment of receipt, even in case of temporary component failures (reliability)
- horizontal scalability of ingestion and processing by adding consumers as manufacturer numbers or update frequency increase (scalability)
- loose coupling between data sources and processing logic, allowing new manufacturers, protocols, or formats to be added by introducing new producers or consumers without modifying the core system (extensibility)
- event-driven processing that naturally supports near-real-time inventory updates, stock history tracking, and stock notification subscriptions
- a maintainable and economical system structure by keeping business logic in a modular monolith while using asynchronous processing only where it provides clear benefits

---

## Accepting that (we will have)
- higher infrastructure and operational complexity compared to a purely synchronous solution, due to operating and monitoring a message broker
- more difficult testing and debugging caused by asynchronous processing flows
- no inherent guarantee of processing order, requiring explicit handling of timestamps and versions at the application level
- additional maintenance effort when inventory schemas change, as affected consumers must be updated accordingly
